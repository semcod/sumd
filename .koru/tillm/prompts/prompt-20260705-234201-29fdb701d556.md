Interactive GUI calibration required (cannot be done headless). Steps for the human at the machine: (1) Focus the Qoder IDE and open its AI chat panel. (2) Place the mouse cursor directly over the chat text-input field and keep it still. (3) Within a terminal run: koru autopilot calibrate --ide qoder   (it captures the pointer position after a 5s --delay-seconds and writes the 'qoder' entry into ~/.koru/ide-os-injector.json). (4) Optionally verify: koru autopilot route --ide auto — the 'no chat calibration for qoder' line should clear. Note: on this GNOME/Wayland session the typing backends are also currently non-viable, so to make the route actually usable you will additionally need to enable a backend (e.g. export KORU_ALLOW_BLIND_KEYBOARD_FALLBACK=1 for ydotool_blind_injection) or connect the qoder IDE plugin.
